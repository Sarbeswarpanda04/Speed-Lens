About Speed Testing

Download Speed

Measures how quickly your device receives data from the internet.

  • What it affects: Streaming videos, downloading files, browsing websites
  • Good speeds: 25+ Mbps for HD streaming, 100+ Mbps for 4K
  • Units: Measured in Megabits per second (Mbps)

Upload Speed

Measures how quickly your device sends data to the internet.

  • What it affects: Video calls, uploading files, live streaming
  • Good speeds: 3+ Mbps for video calls, 10+ Mbps for live streaming
  • Typically: Lower than download speed on most connections

Ping (Latency)

Measures the delay in milliseconds between your device and the server.

  • What it affects: Online gaming, video calls, real-time applications
  • Good latency: Under 20ms excellent, under 50ms good
  • Units: Measured in milliseconds (ms)

Jitter

Measures the variation in ping times, indicating connection stability.

  • What it affects: Voice quality, video smoothness, gaming performance
  • Good jitter: Under 5ms excellent, under 15ms acceptable
  • Lower is better: More consistent connection quality

Pro Tips for Better Results

Use Wired Connection

Ethernet provides more stable and faster speeds than Wi-Fi

Close Other Apps

Stop downloads, streaming, and other bandwidth-heavy activities

Test Multiple Times

Run tests at different times to get a complete picture

Server Location

Closer servers typically provide better speeds and lower ping
