Frequently Asked Questions

Why are my speed test results different from my ISP's advertised speeds?

ISP advertised speeds are often "up to" speeds under ideal conditions. Real-world speeds can vary due to network congestion, distance from servers, WiFi interference, device limitations, and shared bandwidth with other users.

How accurate are the speed test results?

Speed Lens uses industry-standard testing methods and multiple servers to provide accurate results. However, results can vary based on network conditions, server load, and your device's performance. We recommend running multiple tests for the most accurate assessment.

Does Speed Lens work on mobile devices?

Yes! Speed Lens is fully optimized for mobile devices and tablets. It works on iOS, Android, and other mobile platforms through your web browser. You can also install it as a PWA (Progressive Web App) for a native app experience.

Is my data private and secure?

Absolutely! Speed Lens doesn't require registration and stores your test history locally on your device. We don't collect personal information, and all communications are encrypted. See our Privacy Policy for complete details.

Can I export my speed test history?

Yes! You can export your test history in multiple formats including PDF reports, CSV data files, and JSON format. Use the export button in the history section to download your data.

What's the difference between download and upload speeds?

Download speed measures how fast data flows from the internet to your device (streaming, browsing, downloading). Upload speed measures how fast data flows from your device to the internet (uploading files, video calls, posting content). Most internet plans have higher download than upload speeds.
